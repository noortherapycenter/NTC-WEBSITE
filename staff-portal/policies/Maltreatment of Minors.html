Maltreatment of Minors — Mandated Reporting

Policy P-21 Effective: May 24, 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Policy Statement

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear standards and procedures for recognizing, reporting, documenting, and responding to suspected maltreatment of minors receiving Early Intensive Developmental and Behavioral Intervention (EIDBI) services through Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”). This policy exists to:

  • Protect children from abuse, neglect, exploitation, and harm
  • Ensure compliance with Minnesota mandated reporting laws and DHS licensing requirements
  • Promote immediate and appropriate reporting of suspected maltreatment
  • Establish staff accountability and documentation standards
  • Support a safe and trauma-informed service environment for all children receiving EIDBI services

2. Scope

This policy applies to all Noor Therapy Center employees, contractors, interns, trainees, volunteers, and service providers involved in EIDBI services, including but not limited to:

  • Behavioral Technicians (BTs)
  • Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 EIDBI providers
  • Qualified Supervising Professionals (QSPs)
  • Clinical Director
  • Administrative staff
  • Telehealth providers
  • Support personnel with access to client information This policy applies in all service settings, including:
  • Client homes
  • Clinic-based services
  • Community-based services
  • Telehealth sessions
  • Transportation or transitional service settings
  • Any location where EIDBI services are provided This policy applies whenever a staff member witnesses, suspects, receives disclosure of, or has reason to believe that a child receiving services has experienced maltreatment.

3. Definitions

Definitions referenced in this policy follow Minnesota Statutes section 260E.03 and DHS Form DHS-7634E-ENG 7-25.

Maltreatment “Maltreatment” includes:

  • Egregious harm
  • Neglect
  • Physical abuse
  • Sexual abuse
  • Mental injury
  • Threatened injury
  • Substantial child endangerment
  • Maltreatment occurring within a facility or licensed service setting Mandated Reporter:

A mandated reporter is an individual legally required under Minnesota law to report suspected child maltreatment immediately or within 24 hours.

Neglect:

Failure to provide necessary food, shelter, supervision, medical care, education, or protection necessary for a child’s physical or emotional well-being.

Physical Abuse:

Non-accidental physical injury or threatened injury inflicted upon a child. Sexual Abuse:

Any sexual conduct, exploitation, trafficking, or inappropriate sexual contact involving a minor. Mental Injury:

Observable impairment to a child’s emotional functioning, psychological capacity, or behavioral stability.

Threatened Injury:

A condition or action creating substantial risk of physical abuse, sexual abuse, or mental injury. EIDBI Services:

Early Intensive Developmental and Behavioral Intervention services authorized under Minnesota DHS for children with autism spectrum disorder or related conditions.

4. Policy Statement

Noor Therapy Center maintains a zero-tolerance approach toward child maltreatment. All staff are mandated reporters and are legally required to immediately report suspected maltreatment of minors in accordance with Minnesota law and DHS requirements. Staff are not required to investigate or prove abuse or neglect before making a report. Reasonable suspicion alone is sufficient to require reporting.

Noor Therapy Center prohibits retaliation against any person making a good-faith report or participating in an investigation.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, termination, licensing consequences, and/or criminal penalties.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

All Staff All employees and contractors are responsible for:

  • Recognizing signs of possible maltreatment
  • Immediately reporting suspected maltreatment
  • Maintaining confidentiality
  • Completing required mandated reporter training
  • Cooperating with investigations
  • Following all documentation requirements Behavioral Technicians and EIDBI Providers Direct service staff are responsible for:
  • Monitoring client safety during services
  • Reporting concerns observed during home, clinic, telehealth, or community sessions
  • Documenting factual observations appropriately
  • Immediately escalating urgent safety concerns Qualified Supervising Professional (QSP)/Clinical Director The Clinical Director/QSP is responsible for:
  • Providing policy oversight
  • Ensuring staff training compliance
  • Supporting staff during reporting procedures
  • Conducting or assigning internal reviews
  • Monitoring corrective action plans
  • Maintaining required documentation Administrative Leadership Administrative leadership is responsible for:
  • Maintaining personnel training records
  • Ensuring policy accessibility
  • Supporting DHS licensing compliance
  • Assisting with corrective action implementation

6. Procedures and Guidelines

6.1 Recognition of Maltreatment

Staff must remain alert to indicators of abuse, neglect, exploitation, or unsafe environments, including but not limited to:

  • Unexplained injuries
  • Repeated injuries
  • Unsafe living conditions
  • Poor hygiene or malnutrition
  • Extreme fearfulness or withdrawal
  • Sexualized behaviors
  • Exposure to violence or substance abuse
  • Inappropriate caregiver interactions

6.2 Immediate Safety Response

If a child is in immediate danger:

  • Staff must immediately call 911
  • Staff must prioritize child safety
  • Staff should follow organizational emergency response procedures

6.3 External Reporting Requirements

Reports must be made immediately, as soon as possible, and no later than 24 hours after suspicion arises.

6.4 Internal Notification

After an external report is made, staff must notify:

Kelly Ann Clark, Clinical Director/QSP Internal notification may never delay external reporting obligations.

6.5 Documentation Requirements

Staff must document:

  • Objective observations
  • Date and time of observations
  • Date and time report was made
  • Agency contacted
  • Name of intake worker, if available
  • Report/reference number, if provided
  • Immediate protective actions taken Documentation must remain factual and free from assumptions or personal opinions.

6.6 Confidentiality

All reports and documentation are confidential and may only be shared with:

  • Investigative agencies
  • DHS licensing representatives
  • Authorized Noor Therapy Center leadership
  • Individuals directly involved in the investigation process

6.7 Internal Review

Noor Therapy Center will complete an internal review within 30 calendar days after learning of a report.

The review will evaluate:

  • Whether policy and procedures were followed
  • Whether policies were adequate
  • Whether additional training is needed
  • Whether corrective action is necessary
  • Whether similar incidents have occurred previously Person Responsible for Internal Reviews The internal review will be completed by:

Kelly Ann Clark, Clinical Director/QSP If the Clinical Director is involved in the allegation, the review will be completed by: Agency Ownership

6.8 Corrective Action Plans

When necessary, Noor Therapy Center will develop corrective action plans to:

  • Improve safety practices
  • Address policy noncompliance
  • Improve staff performance
  • Prevent future incidents

7. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

  • Verbal or written corrective action
  • Suspension
  • Termination of employment or contract
  • Reporting to licensing or regulatory authorities
  • Criminal penalties under Minnesota law
  • Disqualification from positions involving direct client contact Compliance with this policy will be monitored through:
  • Staff training audits
  • Internal reviews
  • Documentation audits
  • DHS licensing reviews
  • Supervision and quality assurance processes

8. Exceptions

There are no exceptions to mandated reporting obligations under this policy. Questions regarding reporting procedures may be directed to the Clinical Director or administrative leadership; however, consultation may never delay a required report.

9. Legal and Regulatory References

This policy is based upon and governed by:

  • Minnesota Statutes section 260E
  • Minnesota Statutes section 245A
  • Minnesota DHS Licensing Requirements
  • DHS Form DHS-7634E-ENG 7-25
  • EIDBI Provider Manual and DHS service standards
  • Minnesota mandated reporter laws
  • Applicable federal and state confidentiality laws
