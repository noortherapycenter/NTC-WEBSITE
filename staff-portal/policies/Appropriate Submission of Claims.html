Appropriate Submission of Claims

Policy P-13 Effective: January 01, 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Policy

Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”) is committed to ensuring that all claims submitted for Early Intensive Developmental and Behavioral Intervention (EIDBI) services are accurate, complete, timely, and compliant with all applicable federal regulations, Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS) requirements, and Minnesota Medicaid (Medical Assistance) guidelines.

All billing must reflect services that are:

  • Medically necessary
  • Authorized through an approved Individual Treatment Plan (ITP)
  • Delivered by qualified staff within their scope of practice
  • Fully supported by clinical documentation

Noor Therapy Center strictly prohibits fraudulent, abusive, or improper billing practices, including but not limited to:

  • Billing for services not rendered
  • Falsifying documentation
  • Upcoding or unbundling services
  • Duplicate billing
  • Billing beyond authorized units
  • Misrepresenting provider credentials or service delivery

All staff are expected to adhere to ethical billing practices and maintain full compliance with EIDBI standards. Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination, and may be reported to appropriate regulatory authorities.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:

  • Ensure compliance with DHS EIDBI billing requirements
  • Promote accuracy and integrity in all claims submissions
  • Prevent fraud, waste, and abuse
  • Establish clear roles, responsibilities, and procedures for billing activities
  • Support audit readiness and documentation integrity

3. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • Clinical staff responsible for documentation of services
  • The Billing Manager/Owner (Mohammed Mohamed)
  • The Clinical Director (Kelly Ann Clark)
  • Any administrative staff involved in claims processing, submission, or reconciliation

4. Procedure

4.1 Verification of Client Eligibility and Authorization

Prior to the delivery of any service, Noor Therapy Center ensures that:

  • The client has active Minnesota Medicaid (Medical Assistance) eligibility
  • A current and approved Individual Treatment Plan (ITP) is on file
  • Services to be delivered are authorized under EIDBI guidelines

Authorization and eligibility verification is completed prior to initiating services and reviewed periodically to ensure continued compliance.

4.2 Documentation Requirements

All billed services must be supported by accurate and complete documentation entered into Passage Health, the organization’s designated electronic health record system.

Each session note must include, at minimum:

  • Client name and identifying information
  • Date of service
  • Exact start and end times (no rounding or estimation)
  • Location of service (in-home, center, etc.)
  • Service type and corresponding CPT code
  • Name and credential level of the provider
  • Detailed description of interventions implemented
  • Objective data collected
  • Client response to intervention
  • Plan for next session

Documentation must:

  • Be completed on the same day
  • Be completed within 24 hours at maximum
  • Be accurate, legible, and reflect the actual service delivered

Incomplete, late, or inaccurate documentation may result in delayed billing, denial of claims, or corrective action.

4.3 Billing and Claims Submission Process

  • All clinical documentation is entered into Passage Health and reviewed prior to billing.
  • Claims are submitted electronically through Waystar clearinghouse.
  • The Billing Manager (Mohammed Mohamed) is responsible for:
  • Reviewing all claims prior to submission
  • Ensuring alignment between documentation and billed services
  • Verifying correct use of CPT codes, modifiers, and units
  • Confirming that services billed do not exceed authorized limits

Only services that are properly documented and verified may be submitted for reimbursement.

4.4 Timeliness of Claims Submission

Noor Therapy Center adheres to all Minnesota Medicaid and EIDBI billing timelines.

  • Claims are submitted on a routine billing cycle (weekly or bi-weekly)
  • Claims must be submitted within payer-required timeframes
  • Denials are reviewed within 5 business days of receipt
  • Appeals and corrected claims are submitted promptly in accordance with payer requirements

Failure to meet submission timelines may result in loss of reimbursement.

4.5 Internal Auditing and Monitoring

To ensure ongoing compliance, Noor Therapy Center implements the following audit processes:

  • Weekly claim reviews conducted by the Billing Manager
  • Monthly reconciliation audits, comparing:
  • Claims submitted
  • Session logs
  • Clinical documentation in Passage Health

Audits are conducted in coordination with the Clinical Director to ensure both clinical and billing accuracy.

Any discrepancies identified during audits are documented and addressed immediately.

4.6 Error Identification, Correction, and Refunds

If an error is identified in a submitted claim:

  • A corrected claim will be submitted promptly
  • Any overpayments will be refunded in accordance with payer requirements
  • The error will be documented, including:
  • Nature of the error
  • Root cause
  • Corrective action taken

Repeated errors may result in additional staff training, increased monitoring, or disciplinary action.

4.7 Staff Training and Accountability

All staff involved in documentation or billing must receive training on:

  • Documentation standards
  • EIDBI service requirements
  • Ethical billing practices
  • Use of Passage Health

Staff are responsible for ensuring that their documentation supports the services billed.

Failure to comply with documentation and billing requirements may result in:

  • Suspension of billing privileges
  • Additional supervision or retraining
  • Disciplinary action

4.8 Record Retention

All records related to claims submission, including:

  • Clinical documentation
  • Claims and billing records
  • Remittance advice (EOBs)
  • Audit records

will be securely maintained for a minimum of 7 years, in accordance with Minnesota DHS requirements.

5. Responsibility

  • Billing Manager (Mohammed Mohamed): Oversees claims submission, auditing, and compliance
  • Clinical Director (Kelly Ann Clark): Ensures clinical documentation supports billing and aligns with ITPs
  • Clinical Staff: Responsible for accurate, timely, and complete documentation
  • Administrative Staff: Support verification, tracking, and record maintenance

6. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in corrective action, including:

  • Retraining
  • Increased supervision
  • Suspension of duties
  • Termination of employment

Serious violations may be reported to:

  • Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS)
  • Medicaid Program Integrity

7. Review and Revision

This policy will be reviewed annually or as required by changes in DHS, EIDBI, or Medicaid regulations.
