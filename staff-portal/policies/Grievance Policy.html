Complaint & Grievance Procedures

Policy P-23 Effective: January 01, 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Policy

Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”) is committed to providing high quality, ethical, and respectful services. All clients and caregivers have the right to voice concerns, file complaints, and submit grievances regarding any aspect of services without fear of retaliation.

Noor Therapy Center ensures that all grievances are handled promptly, fairly, confidentially, and in accordance with Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS) requirements. The organization maintains a structured grievance process that promotes transparency, accountability, and continuous improvement.

No individual shall be subject to retaliation, discrimination, or adverse treatment for filing a grievance in good faith.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to

  • Ensure a clear and accessible process for submitting grievances
  • Ensure timely and fair resolution of concerns
  • Protect the rights of clients and caregivers
  • Maintain compliance with DHS and EIDBI requirements
  • Identify trends and improve service quality

3. Scope

This policy applies to grievances related to, but not limited to

  • Quality of services provided
  • Staff conduct or professionalism
  • Billing or administrative concerns
  • Communication issues
  • Safety or ethical concerns

This policy applies to

  • Clients receiving services
  • Parents or legal guardians
  • Caregivers
  • Staff members submitting internal concerns

4. Procedure

4.1 Methods for Submitting a Grievance

Grievances may be submitted through the following method

  • Email: info@noortherapycenter.com

Grievances may be submitted at any time and should include

  • Name of the individual submitting the grievance (optional if anonymous)
  • Description of the concern
  • Date or dates of the incident if applicable
  • Any supporting information

Anonymous grievances will be accepted. However, lack of identifying information may limit the ability to fully investigate.

4.2 Acknowledgment of Grievance

  • All grievances will be acknowledged within 3 business days of receipt
  • Acknowledgment will include confirmation that the grievance has been received and is under review

4.3 Investigation Process

All grievances will be reviewed and investigated by designated leadership, including

  • CEO Mohammed Mohamed
  • Clinical Director Kelly Ann Clark

The investigation process may include

  • Review of documentation such as case notes and supervision records
  • Interviews with involved staff or individuals
  • Review of relevant policies and procedures

Investigations will be conducted in a fair, objective, and timely manner

4.4 Resolution Timeline

  • Noor Therapy Center aims to resolve all grievances within 30 calendar days
  • If additional time is required, the individual who submitted the grievance will be notified and provided with an updated timeline

4.5 Outcome and Notification

Upon completion of the investigation

  • A determination will be made based on findings
  • Appropriate corrective actions will be implemented if necessary
  • The individual who submitted the grievance will be notified of the outcome to the extent permitted by confidentiality laws

4.6 Corrective Actions

If a grievance is substantiated, corrective actions may include

  • Staff retraining
  • Increased supervision or oversight
  • Policy or procedure updates
  • Disciplinary action if applicable

All corrective actions must be documented and tracked to completion

4.7 Appeal Process

If the individual submitting the grievance is not satisfied with the outcome, they may request an appeal

  • Appeals will be reviewed by an alternate member of leadership not directly involved in the initial investigation
  • A final determination will be made and documented

4.8 Documentation and Recordkeeping

All grievances must be documented and maintained, including

  • Original complaint
  • Date received
  • Investigation steps taken
  • Findings and outcome
  • Corrective actions implemented

All grievance records will be securely maintained and retained for a minimum of 7 years in accordance with Minnesota DHS requirements

5. Responsibility

  • CEO, Mohammed Mohamed oversees grievance intake, tracking, and resolution
  • Clinical Director Kelly Ann Clark supports investigation and clinical oversight
  • All staff are responsible for cooperating with grievance investigations and maintaining professionalism

6. Compliance and Enforcement

  • Failure to comply with this policy may result in corrective action, retraining, or disciplinary measures
  • Serious concerns may be reported to the Minnesota Department of Human Services or other regulatory authorities if required
