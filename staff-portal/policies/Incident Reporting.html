Incident Reporting & Response

Policy P-19 Effective: July 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Purpose

To ensure injuries, accidents, behavioral emergencies, medication events, property damage, and near-misses are documented, reviewed, and used to prevent recurrence.

2. Policy

  • Any incident involving a client, staff member, or visitor — injury, aggression resulting in harm, elopement, medical event, medication error, property damage, or a credible near-miss — must be documented on the Incident Report form before the end of the shift.
  • Client injuries are communicated to the parent/guardian the same day by the supervising QSP or designee.
  • Incident reports are internal quality documents: factual, objective, and free of blame or speculation. They are filed separately from the client's clinical record.
  • The leadership team reviews all incident reports monthly for patterns and corrective action.

3. Procedure

  1. Respond first: ensure safety, give first aid, call 911 if needed.
  2. Notify: tell the supervising QSP or site lead immediately; they determine parent/guardian and any external notifications (county, licensing, mandated report).
  3. Document: the staff member who witnessed the incident completes the Incident Report the same day — who, what, when, where, response given, and follow-up needed.
  4. Review: the supervisor reviews and signs within 2 business days and routes to the Executive Director for any incident involving injury, elopement, or emergency services.
  5. Follow up: corrective actions (environment changes, protocol updates, retraining) are assigned an owner and a due date.
