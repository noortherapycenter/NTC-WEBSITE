Emergency Procedures

Policy P-20 Effective: July 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Purpose

To prepare staff to respond quickly and consistently to fires, medical emergencies, severe weather, and security threats at the center and during in-home services.

2. Policy

  • Emergency exit maps are posted in every room. Staff review evacuation routes, the severe-weather shelter area, and assembly point during orientation.
  • First aid kits and fire extinguishers are inspected monthly (documented on the Supply Inventory).
  • Staff maintain current emergency contact information for every client on site; attendance records must make an accurate head count possible at all times.
  • Fire and severe-weather drills are conducted and logged at least twice per year.

3. Procedure

  1. Fire: evacuate immediately using the nearest exit, take the attendance sheet, escort clients to the assembly point, count heads, call 911. Never re-enter until cleared.
  2. Medical emergency: call 911 for any loss of consciousness, seizure lasting over 5 minutes (or per the client's seizure plan), difficulty breathing, or serious injury. Give first aid within training. Notify guardian immediately.
  3. Severe weather: on a tornado warning, move everyone to the designated interior shelter area away from windows; remain until the warning clears.
  4. Security threat: lock doors, move clients out of sight, silence devices, call 911 when safe. Staff do not confront intruders.
  5. In-home sessions: know the address and nearest exit at every home visit; if the environment becomes unsafe, end the session and call 911 and your supervisor.
  6. Always: complete an Incident Report the same day for any emergency response.
