Fraud, Waste & Abuse Prevention

Policy P-15 Effective: July 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Purpose

To prevent, detect, and respond to fraud, waste, and abuse (FWA) in the delivery and billing of EIDBI services, consistent with the federal and Minnesota False Claims Acts.

2. Policy

  • Noor Therapy Center bills only for medically necessary services actually delivered as documented, within active authorizations, by properly enrolled and supervised providers.
  • Examples of prohibited conduct: billing for services not rendered; falsifying or pre-writing documentation; upcoding provider level or service type; billing during a lapsed authorization or supervision requirement; paying or receiving anything of value for referrals (anti-kickback).
  • All staff receive FWA training at hire and annually.
  • Staff must report suspected FWA. Reports may be made to the Executive Director anonymously, and good-faith reporters are protected from retaliation (whistleblower protections).
  • Confirmed overpayments are reported and repaid to the payer promptly.

3. Procedure

  1. Prevent: pre-billing reconciliation (see Service Verification), enrollment/affiliation checks, and supervision tracking.
  2. Detect: quarterly internal audits sample claims against documentation; anomalies (impossible schedules, duplicate times, outlier hours) are investigated.
  3. Report: staff report suspected FWA immediately; the Executive Director investigates, documents findings, and involves counsel as needed.
  4. Respond: substantiated FWA results in discipline up to termination, repayment to payers, and required reporting to DHS/appropriate authorities. Suspected FWA may also be reported to the Minnesota DHS Office of Inspector General.
