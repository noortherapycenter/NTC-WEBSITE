Documentation of Staff Qualifications

Policy P-07 Effective: January 01, 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Policy

Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”) ensures that all staff providing Early Intensive Developmental and Behavioral Intervention (EIDBI) services meet all applicable qualification requirements as defined by the Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS), EIDBI service standards, and relevant state and federal regulations.

All staff qualifications must be verified, documented, and maintained prior to the provision of any services. Noor Therapy Center strictly prohibits any individual from providing services without verified credentials, completed background studies, and required documentation on file.

The organization maintains complete, accurate, and up-to-date personnel records to demonstrate compliance with DHS licensing, audit, and program integrity requirements.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:

  • Ensure compliance with DHS EIDBI provider qualification requirements
  • Verify that all staff meet minimum standards for their role
  • Maintain accurate and complete personnel records
  • Protect client safety and service quality
  • Ensure readiness for audits, reviews, and licensing requirements

3. Scope

This policy applies to all individuals employed or contracted by Noor Therapy Center, including:

  • All provider levels (Level I, Level II, Level III)
  • Qualified Supervising Professional (QSP) / Advanced Clinical Practitioner (ACH)
  • Administrative and billing staff

4. Procedure

4.1 Pre-Employment and Pre-Service Verification

Prior to hire and before any service delivery, Noor Therapy Center verifies that all staff meet required qualifications for their role.

Verification includes:

  • Review of education and degree requirements
  • Confirmation of certifications (if applicable)
  • Verification of relevant experience
  • Completion of required background studies

No staff member may begin work or provide services until all required qualifications have been verified and documented.

4.2 Required Documentation for Personnel Files

Each employee must have a complete personnel file containing, at minimum, the following documentation:

1. Identification and Employment Records

  • Completed job application or resume
  • Proof of identity and employment eligibility

2. Education and Credential Verification

  • Copies of diplomas or transcripts verifying degree completion
  • Certification documentation (if applicable, e.g., RBT, BCBA, etc.)
  • Verification of credentials through appropriate certifying bodies

3. DHS and Background Study Requirements

  • Completed NETStudy 2.0 background study with eligible or set-aside determination
  • Documentation of clearance prior to providing services

4. Required Training Documentation

  • Proof of completion of all required initial training
  • Certificates for DHS/TrainLink trainings (e.g., EIDBI 101, Cultural Responsiveness)
  • Mandated reporter training documentation

5. Health and Safety Requirements (if applicable)

  • CPR and First Aid certification (if required for role)
  • Any additional health or safety clearances required by DHS

6. Supervision and Role Documentation

  • Documentation of assigned roles (L1, L2, L3)
  • Supervision structure and assigned supervisor
  • Evidence of supervision requirements being met (as applicable)

4.3 Credential Verification and Monitoring

Noor Therapy Center ensures that all credentials remain valid and up to date.

  • Credentials are verified through primary sources (e.g., licensing boards, certification registries)
  • Expiration dates are tracked and monitored
  • Staff are notified in advance of upcoming expirations

Staff are responsible for maintaining active credentials and providing updated documentation prior to expiration.

Failure to maintain active credentials will result in immediate suspension from service delivery.

4.4 Role-Based Qualification Requirements

All staff must meet DHS-defined qualifications for their assigned role, including:

  • L1, L2, L3 Providers Must meet minimum education and training requirements and operate under supervision of QSP
  • QSP/ACH (Kelly Ann Clark): Must meet DHS requirements for Qualified Supervising Professionals, including oversight of clinical services and supervision

Noor Therapy Center ensures that staff only perform duties within their defined scope of practice.

4.5 Restrictions on Service Delivery

Staff are prohibited from providing services if:

  • Required documentation is missing or incomplete
  • Background study clearance has not been obtained
  • Credentials have expired or cannot be verified
  • Required training has not been completed

Any lapse in qualification status results in immediate removal from service delivery until resolved.

4.6 Record Maintenance and Security

  • Personnel files are maintained in a secure and confidential manner
  • Access is limited to authorized personnel only
  • Records may be stored electronically and/or physically, with appropriate safeguards

All records must be readily available for review during audits or licensing inspections.

4.7 Record Retention

All personnel and qualification records will be retained for a minimum of 7 years after termination of employment, in accordance with Minnesota DHS requirements.

5. Responsibility

  • Clinical Director (Kelly Ann Clark): Ensures clinical staff meet qualification and supervision requirements
  • Billing Manager / Administration (Mohammed Mohamed): Oversees verification, tracking, and maintenance of personnel records
  • Staff Members: Responsible for providing accurate documentation and maintaining active credentials

6. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to comply with qualification requirements may result in:

  • Immediate suspension from service delivery
  • Corrective action or retraining
  • Disciplinary action, up to and including termination

Serious violations may be reported to:

  • Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS)
  • Licensing and regulatory agencies
