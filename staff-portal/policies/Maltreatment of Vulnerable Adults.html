Maltreatment of Vulnerable Adults — Mandated Reporting

Policy P-22 Effective: May 24, 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Purpose Statement

The purpose of this policy is to establish standards and procedures for recognizing, reporting, documenting, and responding to suspected maltreatment of vulnerable adults receiving services through Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”).

This policy exists to:

  • Protect vulnerable adults from abuse, neglect, financial exploitation, and harm
  • Ensure compliance with Minnesota mandated reporting laws and DHS requirements
  • Establish staff responsibilities for reporting suspected maltreatment
  • Promote immediate intervention and protective action
  • Maintain safe, ethical, and trauma-informed service environments
  • Ensure organizational accountability and compliance with state regulations

2. Scope

This policy applies to all Noor Therapy Center employees, contractors, interns, trainees, volunteers, and service providers who provide services to vulnerable adults, including but not limited to:

  • Behavioral Technicians (BTs)
  • Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 providers
  • Qualified Supervising Professionals (QSPs)
  • Clinical Director
  • Administrative staff
  • Telehealth providers
  • Support staff with access to client information

This policy applies in all service settings, including:

  • Client homes
  • Community-based services
  • Clinic-based services
  • Telehealth services
  • Transportation or transitional settings
  • Any environment where services are provided

This policy applies whenever staff witness, suspect, receive disclosure of, or have reason to believe that a vulnerable adult has experienced maltreatment.

3. Definitions

Definitions referenced in this policy follow Minnesota Statutes sections 626.557 and 626.5572.

Vulnerable Adult

A vulnerable adult is an individual age 18 or older who:

  • Receives services from a licensed facility or provider; or
  • Possesses physical, mental, emotional, or developmental impairments that limit the individual’s ability to protect themselves from maltreatment.

Maltreatment

“Maltreatment” includes:

  • Abuse
  • Neglect
  • Financial exploitation

Abuse

Any act that causes physical pain, injury, emotional distress, sexual abuse, unreasonable confinement, or deprivation of services necessary to maintain physical or mental health.

Neglect

Failure or omission by a caregiver or service provider to provide care, supervision, food, clothing, shelter, health care, or services necessary to maintain the vulnerable adult’s health or safety.

Financial Exploitation

The unauthorized or improper use of a vulnerable adult’s funds, property, assets, or resources.

Mandated Reporter

A mandated reporter is an individual legally required to report suspected maltreatment of a vulnerable adult under Minnesota law.

4. Policy Statement

Noor Therapy Center maintains a zero-tolerance policy regarding maltreatment of vulnerable adults.

All staff are mandated reporters and are legally required to immediately report known or suspected maltreatment of vulnerable adults in accordance with Minnesota law.

Reports must be made immediately, and no later than 24 hours after the incident or suspicion arises.

Staff are not responsible for investigating or proving maltreatment before making a report. Reasonable suspicion alone requires reporting.

Noor Therapy Center prohibits retaliation against any individual who makes a good-faith report or participates in an investigation.

Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, termination, licensing consequences, and/or criminal penalties.

5. Roles and Responsibilities

All Staff

All employees and contractors are responsible for:

  • Recognizing signs of maltreatment
  • Immediately reporting suspected maltreatment
  • Maintaining confidentiality
  • Completing required training
  • Cooperating with investigations
  • Following documentation requirements

Direct Service Providers

Direct care and service staff are responsible for:

  • Monitoring client safety during services
  • Reporting concerns observed during home, clinic, telehealth, or community sessions
  • Responding appropriately to immediate safety concerns
  • Completing factual documentation

Clinical Director/QSP

The Clinical Director/QSP is responsible for:

  • Providing policy oversight
  • Supporting staff with reporting procedures
  • Monitoring staff compliance
  • Conducting or assigning internal reviews
  • Ensuring corrective actions are implemented
  • Maintaining policy accessibility

Administrative Leadership

Administrative leadership is responsible for:

  • Maintaining training records
  • Supporting DHS compliance requirements
  • Monitoring policy implementation
  • Maintaining documentation standards

6. Procedures and Guidelines

6.1 Recognition of Maltreatment

Staff must remain alert to indicators of abuse, neglect, or exploitation, including but not limited to:

  • Unexplained injuries
  • Sudden behavioral or emotional changes
  • Fearfulness around caregivers
  • Unsafe living conditions
  • Malnutrition or poor hygiene
  • Lack of necessary medical care
  • Unusual financial transactions or missing belongings
  • Isolation or unreasonable restraint
  • Signs of emotional abuse or intimidation

6.2 Immediate Safety Response

If a vulnerable adult is in immediate danger:

  • Staff must immediately call 911
  • Staff must prioritize client safety
  • Emergency procedures must be followed

6.3 External Reporting Requirements

If you are a mandated reporter and know or suspect maltreatment of a vulnerable adult, you must report it immediately and no later than 24 hours.

Reports may also be made internally to:

Kelly Ann Clark, Clinical Director/QSP

If the person listed above is involved in the alleged or suspected maltreatment, reports must be made to:

Agency Ownership or Designated Administrative Leadership

6.4 Internal Reporting Process

When an internal report is received, the Clinical Director/QSP is responsible for determining whether the report must be forwarded to the Minnesota Adult Abuse Reporting Center (MAARC).

If the Clinical Director/QSP is involved in the alleged maltreatment, Agency Ownership or Designated Administrative Leadership will assume responsibility for determining whether the report must be forwarded.

Any required external report must be forwarded within 24 hours.

If a staff member reports internally, they will receive written notice within two working days indicating whether the report was forwarded to MAARC. The notice must protect the reporter’s identity and inform the reporter that they may independently contact MAARC if dissatisfied with the internal determination.

6.5 Documentation Requirements

Staff must document:

  • Objective observations
  • Date and time of observations
  • Date and time the report was made
  • Agency contacted
  • Name of intake worker, if available
  • Report or reference number, if available
  • Protective actions taken

Documentation must remain factual and free from assumptions or personal opinions.

6.6 Confidentiality

All reports and related documentation are confidential.

Information may only be shared with:

  • Investigative agencies
  • DHS licensing representatives
  • Authorized Noor Therapy Center leadership
  • Individuals directly involved in the investigation process

Unauthorized disclosure of information is prohibited.

6.7 Internal Review

When Noor Therapy Center has reason to know that an internal or external report of alleged or suspected maltreatment has been made, the organization will complete an internal review within 30 calendar days.

The review will evaluate whether:

  • Related policies and procedures were followed
  • Policies and procedures were adequate
  • Additional staff training is needed
  • The event is similar to past incidents
  • Corrective action is necessary to protect vulnerable adults

6.8 Primary and Secondary Persons Responsible for Internal Review

The internal review will be completed by:

Kelly Ann Clark, Clinical Director/QSP

If this individual is involved in the alleged or suspected maltreatment, the review will be completed by:

Agency Ownership or Designated Administrative Leadership

6.9 Documentation of Internal Review

Noor Therapy Center will document completion of all internal reviews and make documentation immediately accessible to the Commissioner upon request.

6.10 Corrective Action Plan

Based on the results of the internal review, Noor Therapy Center will develop, document, and implement corrective action plans designed to:

  • Correct current lapses
  • Improve safety practices
  • Prevent future incidents
  • Improve staff compliance and performance

7. Staff Training

The license holder shall ensure that each new mandated reporter receives orientation within 72 hours of first providing direct-contact services to a vulnerable adult and annually thereafter.

Training shall include:

  • Reporting requirements under Minnesota Statutes sections 626.557 and 626.5572
  • Requirements under Minnesota Statutes section 245A.65
  • Noor Therapy Center’s abuse prevention plan
  • Internal policies and procedures
  • Recognition of abuse, neglect, and financial exploitation
  • Confidentiality and documentation requirements

The organization will document completion of all training and maintain records in personnel files.

This policy will remain readily accessible to staff in accordance with Minnesota Statutes section 245A.04, subdivision 14.

8. Compliance and Enforcement

Failure to comply with this policy may result in:

  • Corrective action
  • Suspension
  • Termination of employment or contract
  • Reporting to licensing or regulatory agencies
  • Criminal penalties under Minnesota law
  • Disqualification from positions involving direct client contact

Compliance will be monitored through:

  • Training audits
  • Documentation reviews
  • Internal reviews
  • Supervision and quality assurance activities
  • DHS licensing reviews

9. Exceptions

There are no exceptions to mandated reporting requirements under this policy.

Consultation with supervisors or leadership may occur but may never delay a required report.
