Discharge & Transition Planning

Policy P-03 Effective: July 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Purpose

To ensure clients leave services in a planned, supported way — whether they have met their goals, aged out, moved, or transitioned to another provider.

2. Policy

  • Discharge planning begins at admission: the ITP identifies anticipated discharge criteria and transition goals.
  • Planned discharge occurs when goals are met, when a less intensive service level is appropriate, when the client ages out of EIDBI eligibility, or at the family's request.
  • Noor Therapy Center does not abandon clients: when the agency initiates discharge (e.g., inability to meet needs, persistent non-participation, safety concerns), the family receives written notice with reasonable transition time and referrals to alternative providers.
  • A written discharge/transition summary is completed for every discharge and provided to the family and, with a signed release, to receiving providers.

3. Procedure

  1. Plan: review discharge criteria at every ITP review; discuss upcoming transitions (school entry, aging out) with the family well in advance.
  2. Prepare: fade services per the plan; train caregivers and receiving programs on effective strategies.
  3. Summarize: the QSP completes a discharge summary within 30 days: services provided, progress on goals, recommendations, and referrals.
  4. Close: end authorizations correctly, complete final billing, and file the record per the retention schedule.
