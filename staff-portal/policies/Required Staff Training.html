Required Staff Training

Policy P-08 Effective: January 01, 2026 Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. (DBA Noor Therapy Center)

1. Policy

Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”) is committed to ensuring that all staff are properly trained, competent, and qualified to deliver Early Intensive Developmental and Behavioral Intervention (EIDBI) services in accordance with Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS) requirements, applicable federal and state regulations, and best practices in Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA).

All staff must complete required training prior to providing services and participate in ongoing training and supervision to maintain competency. Training is designed to ensure that services are delivered safely, ethically, and effectively in an in-home setting.

No staff member may provide services independently until all required training and competency requirements have been met and documented.

2. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to:

  • Ensure compliance with DHS EIDBI training requirements
  • Promote safe and effective service delivery
  • Establish standardized training expectations for all staff
  • Maintain staff competency in clinical, ethical, and operational areas
  • Support ongoing professional development and quality improvement

3. Scope

This policy applies to:

  • All provider levels (L1, L2, & L3)
  • Qualified Supervising Professional (QSP) / Advanced Certification Holder (ACH)
  • Administrative and billing staff (as applicable to their roles)

4. Procedure

4.1 Pre-Service (Initial) Training Requirements

All newly hired staff must complete all required training prior to engaging in any direct or indirect service delivery, including observation, shadowing, or documentation activities. This requirement ensures that all staff meet Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS) and EIDBI standards for competency, safety, and compliance.

Staff are strictly prohibited from providing any services until all required training has been completed, verified, and documented in their personnel file.

Required Initial Training Includes, at Minimum:

1. HIPAA and Client Confidentiality

  • Protection of Protected Health Information (PHI)
  • Secure handling, storage, and transmission of client records
  • Confidential communication practices in home and community settings

2. Mandated Reporting – Minors and Vulnerable Adults

  • Maltreatment of minors (child abuse and neglect)
  • Maltreatment of vulnerable adults, including abuse, neglect, and exploitation
  • Legal obligations as a mandated reporter under Minnesota law
  • Procedures for immediate reporting and documentation
  • Understanding timelines and reporting channels

3. ABA Ethics and Professional Conduct

  • Adherence to ethical standards in Applied Behavior Analysis
  • Maintaining professional boundaries with clients and families
  • Avoidance of dual relationships and conflicts of interest
  • Appropriate conduct in home-based service environments

4. Documentation and Case Note Standards

  • Accurate, objective, and timely documentation practices
  • Requirements for entering notes in Passage Health
  • Alignment of documentation with billing and authorization requirements
  • Ensuring documentation reflects services delivered

5. Behavior Intervention and Safety Procedures

  • Implementation of individualized behavior intervention plans
  • De-escalation strategies and crisis prevention techniques
  • Safe response to challenging behaviors
  • Maintaining safety for clients, staff, and caregivers in the home

6. Emergency Procedures (In-Home Setting)

  • Responding to medical emergencies
  • Fire safety and environmental hazards
  • Emergency communication and escalation procedures
  • Use of emergency contacts and reporting protocols

7. EIDBI-Specific Required Training (TrainLink & DHS-Aligned)

All staff must complete required foundational EIDBI trainings, including but not limited to:

  • EIDBI 101 for Providers
  • Overview of EIDBI services, structure, and expectations
  • Roles and responsibilities within the EIDBI model
  • Cultural Responsiveness Training
  • Delivering culturally responsive and respectful care
  • Understanding diverse family systems and backgrounds
  • Addressing cultural considerations in service delivery
  • Coordinating Services for a Child with Autism
  • Collaboration with caregivers and interdisciplinary teams
  • Supporting continuity of care
  • Understanding systems of care and service coordination

These trainings must be completed through DHS-approved platforms (e.g., TrainLink) or equivalent approved training sources.

8. Overview of EIDBI Services and Provider Roles

  • Understanding EIDBI service types (e.g., direct services, O&D, caregiver training)
  • Scope of practice for each provider level
  • Expectations for supervision and service delivery

4.2 Ongoing and Annual Training Requirements

All staff are required to complete ongoing training to maintain competency and compliance.

  • Annual training is required for all core topics, including:
  • HIPAA and confidentiality
  • Mandated reporting
  • Ethics
  • Documentation standards
  • Additional training is provided based on:
  • Updates to DHS or EIDBI requirements
  • Identified areas of improvement
  • Client-specific clinical needs

Failure to complete required training may result in removal from service delivery until requirements are met.

4.3 Supervision and Clinical Training

Ongoing supervision is a critical component of staff training and competency.

  • Weekly supervision is provided by the QSP/ACH (Kelly Ann Clark)
  • Supervision includes:
  • Direct observation of service delivery
  • Performance feedback
  • Coaching on intervention implementation
  • Review of data collection and documentation
  • Observation & Direction (O&D) is conducted in alignment with EIDBI expectations and proportionate to approximately 20% of direct service hours

Supervision ensures staff are implementing interventions correctly and maintaining clinical integrity.

4.4 Competency Assessment

Staff competency is evaluated on an ongoing basis through:

  • Direct observation by the QSP/ACH
  • Review of session notes and documentation
  • Performance feedback and supervision sessions

Staff must demonstrate competency in:

  • Implementation of behavior intervention plans
  • Accurate data collection
  • Professional conduct and communication
  • Compliance with documentation standards

If a staff member does not meet competency expectations:

  • Additional training will be provided
  • Increased supervision will be implemented
  • Continued deficiencies may result in disciplinary action

4.5 Training Documentation

Noor Therapy Center maintains detailed records of all staff training.

Training records include:

  • Training topic
  • Date of completion
  • Method of training (e.g., in-person, virtual)
  • Trainer name
  • Proof of completion (certificate, attendance log, or competency checklist)

All training documentation is maintained in the employee’s personnel file and is available for audit or review.

4.6 Responsibility and Oversight

  • Clinical Director (Kelly Ann Clark):
  • Oversees clinical training content
  • Ensures training aligns with EIDBI and ABA standards
  • Provides supervision and competency evaluation
  • Administrative Oversight:
  • Tracks training completion
  • Maintains training records
  • Ensures staff compliance with training requirements
  • Staff Members:
  • Responsible for completing all required training
  • Must actively participate in supervision and competency development

4.7 Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with training requirements may result in:

  • Suspension from providing services
  • Additional training or supervision requirements
  • Disciplinary action, up to and including termination

Staff will not be permitted to continue service delivery until all training deficiencies are resolved.

5. Record Retention

All training records will be securely maintained for a minimum of 7 years, in accordance with Minnesota DHS requirements.

6. Compliance and Enforcement

Noor Therapy Center enforces strict adherence to training requirements to ensure client safety, service quality, and regulatory compliance.

Non-compliance may be subject to internal disciplinary action and may be reported if required under regulatory guidelines.
