[H2] 1. Policy
[P] Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”) ensures that all staff providing Early Intensive Developmental and Behavioral Intervention (EIDBI) services meet all applicable qualification requirements as defined by the Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS), EIDBI service standards, and relevant state and federal regulations.
[P] All staff qualifications must be verified, documented, and maintained prior to the provision of any services. Noor Therapy Center strictly prohibits any individual from providing services without verified credentials, completed background studies, and required documentation on file.
[P] The organization maintains complete, accurate, and up-to-date personnel records to demonstrate compliance with DHS licensing, audit, and program integrity requirements.
[H2] 2. Purpose
[P] The purpose of this policy is to:
[LI] Ensure compliance with DHS EIDBI provider qualification requirements
[LI] Verify that all staff meet minimum standards for their role
[LI] Maintain accurate and complete personnel records
[LI] Protect client safety and service quality
[LI] Ensure readiness for audits, reviews, and licensing requirements
[H2] 3. Scope
[P] This policy applies to all individuals employed or contracted by Noor Therapy Center, including:
[LI] All provider levels (Level I, Level II, Level III)
[LI] Qualified Supervising Professional (QSP) / Advanced Clinical Practitioner (ACH)
[LI] Administrative and billing staff
[H2] 4. Procedure
[H3] 4.1 Pre-Employment and Pre-Service Verification
[P] Prior to hire and before any service delivery, Noor Therapy Center verifies that all staff meet required qualifications for their role.
[P] Verification includes:
[LI] Review of education and degree requirements
[LI] Confirmation of certifications (if applicable)
[LI] Verification of relevant experience
[LI] Completion of required background studies
[P] No staff member may begin work or provide services until all required qualifications have been verified and documented.
[H3] 4.2 Required Documentation for Personnel Files
[P] Each employee must have a complete personnel file containing, at minimum, the following documentation:
[P] 1. Identification and Employment Records
[LI] Completed job application or resume
[LI] Proof of identity and employment eligibility
[P] 2. Education and Credential Verification
[LI] Copies of diplomas or transcripts verifying degree completion
[LI] Certification documentation (if applicable, e.g., RBT, BCBA, etc.)
[LI] Verification of credentials through appropriate certifying bodies
[P] 3. DHS and Background Study Requirements
[LI] Completed NETStudy 2.0 background study with eligible or set-aside determination
[LI] Documentation of clearance prior to providing services
[P] 4. Required Training Documentation
[LI] Proof of completion of all required initial training
[LI] Certificates for DHS/TrainLink trainings (e.g., EIDBI 101, Cultural Responsiveness)
[LI] Mandated reporter training documentation
[P] 5. Health and Safety Requirements (if applicable)
[LI] CPR and First Aid certification (if required for role)
[LI] Any additional health or safety clearances required by DHS
[P] 6. Supervision and Role Documentation
[LI] Documentation of assigned roles (L1, L2, L3)
[LI] Supervision structure and assigned supervisor
[LI] Evidence of supervision requirements being met (as applicable)
[H3] 4.3 Credential Verification and Monitoring
[P] Noor Therapy Center ensures that all credentials remain valid and up to date.
[LI] Credentials are verified through primary sources (e.g., licensing boards, certification registries)
[LI] Expiration dates are tracked and monitored
[LI] Staff are notified in advance of upcoming expirations
[P] Staff are responsible for maintaining active credentials and providing updated documentation prior to expiration.
[P] Failure to maintain active credentials will result in immediate suspension from service delivery.
[H3] 4.4 Role-Based Qualification Requirements
[P] All staff must meet DHS-defined qualifications for their assigned role, including:
[LI] L1, L2, L3 Providers Must meet minimum education and training requirements and operate under supervision of QSP
[LI] QSP/ACH (Kelly Ann Clark): Must meet DHS requirements for Qualified Supervising Professionals, including oversight of clinical services and supervision
[P] Noor Therapy Center ensures that staff only perform duties within their defined scope of practice.
[H3] 4.5 Restrictions on Service Delivery
[P] Staff are prohibited from providing services if:
[LI] Required documentation is missing or incomplete
[LI] Background study clearance has not been obtained
[LI] Credentials have expired or cannot be verified
[LI] Required training has not been completed
[P] Any lapse in qualification status results in immediate removal from service delivery until resolved.
[H3] 4.6 Record Maintenance and Security
[LI] Personnel files are maintained in a secure and confidential manner
[LI] Access is limited to authorized personnel only
[LI] Records may be stored electronically and/or physically, with appropriate safeguards
[P] All records must be readily available for review during audits or licensing inspections.
[H3] 4.7 Record Retention
[P] All personnel and qualification records will be retained for a minimum of 7 years after termination of employment, in accordance with Minnesota DHS requirements.
[H2] 5. Responsibility
[LI] Clinical Director (Kelly Ann Clark): Ensures clinical staff meet qualification and supervision requirements
[LI] Billing Manager / Administration (Mohammed Mohamed): Oversees verification, tracking, and maintenance of personnel records
[LI] Staff Members: Responsible for providing accurate documentation and maintaining active credentials
[H2] 6. Compliance and Enforcement

[LI] Immediate suspension from service delivery
[LI] Corrective action or retraining

[LI] Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS)

[P] DOCUMENTATION OF SERVICE ACTIVITY
[H2] 1. Policy
[P] Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”) requires that all service activities provided under Early Intensive Developmental and Behavioral Intervention (EIDBI) are documented accurately, completely, and in a timely manner in accordance with Minnesota Department of Human Services (DHS) requirements, EIDBI service standards, and Medicaid billing regulations.
[P] All documentation must clearly reflect the services delivered, support medical necessity, and align with the client’s approved Individual Treatment Plan (ITP). Documentation serves as the legal and clinical record of services provided and must be sufficient to support billing, audits, and quality assurance.
[P] Failure to properly document services may result in denied claims, recoupment of funds, and disciplinary action.
[H2] 2. Purpose
[P] The purpose of this policy is to:
[LI] Ensure compliance with DHS EIDBI documentation requirements
[LI] Establish standardized expectations for service documentation
[LI] Support accurate billing and claims submission
[LI] Promote high-quality clinical care and continuity of services
[LI] Maintain audit-ready records at all times
[H2] 3. Scope
[P] This policy applies to all staff responsible for documenting services, including:
[LI] Behavior Technicians (BTs)
[LI] Level 1 Providers
[LI] Qualified Supervising Professional (QSP/ACH)
[H2] 4. Procedure
[H3] 4.1 General Documentation Standards
[P] All documentation must be:
[LI] Accurate – Reflecting the actual services provided
[LI] Complete – Including all required elements
[LI] Objective – Based on observable and measurable behavior
[LI] Timely – Completed within required timeframes
[LI] Legible and professional – Free of vague or subjective language
[P] Documentation must be entered into Passage Health, the organization’s designated electronic health record system.
[H3] 4.2 Required Components of Session Notes
[P] Each service session must include a corresponding case note that contains, at minimum:
[LI] Client name and identifying information
[LI] Date of service
[LI] Exact start and end times (no rounding or estimation)
[LI] Total duration of service
[LI] Location of service (in-home, clinic, office, etc.)
[LI] Type of service provided (e.g., 97153, 97155, 97156)
[LI] Name and credential level of the provider
[LI] Goals and objectives targeted during the session
[LI] Description of interventions implemented
[LI] Objective data collected (e.g., frequency, duration, accuracy)
[LI] Client response to interventions
[LI] Any significant events, behaviors, or incidents
[LI] Plan for next session or ongoing treatment
[P] All entries must align with the approved ITP and reflect medically necessary services.
[H3] 4.3 Timeliness of Documentation
[LI] Session notes must be completed on the same day of service whenever possible
[LI] All notes must be completed within 24 hours of service delivery
[LI] Late entries must be clearly identified and justified
[P] Failure to complete documentation within required timelines may result in:
[LI] Delayed or denied billing
[LI] Increased supervision
[LI] Disciplinary action
[H3] 4.4 Data Collection and Integrity
[P] Staff are required to collect and maintain accurate data during each session.
[LI] Data must be recorded in real time whenever possible
[LI] Data must align with treatment goals and intervention plans
[LI] Data must be stored securely within Passage Health or approved systems
[P] The organization may implement periodic checks for:
[LI] Data accuracy
[LI] Consistency across providers
[H3] 4.5 Supervision and Observation & Direction (O&D) Documentation
[P] All supervision activities must be documented in accordance with EIDBI requirements.
[LI] Observation & Direction (O&D) must be documented for each occurrence
[LI] Documentation must include:
[LI] Type of supervision provided
[LI] Activities conducted (e.g., modeling, feedback, protocol modification)
[LI] Staff performance and response
[LI] O&D must occur in proportion to approximately 20% of the client’s direct service hours
[P] Supervision documentation must clearly support clinical oversight and staff development.
[H3] 4.6 Alignment with Billing Requirements
[P] All documentation must directly support claims submitted for reimbursement.
[LI] Units billed must match documented time
[LI] Services billed must match services documented
[LI] Provider credentials must align with the service delivered
[P] Any discrepancy between documentation and billing may result in claim denial or recoupment.
[H3] 4.7 Documentation of Significant Events
[P] Staff must document any significant or unusual events, including but not limited to:
[LI] Aggressive or self-injurious behavior
[LI] Elopement or safety concerns
[LI] Injuries or incidents
[LI] Emergency situations
[P] Such documentation must be factual, objective, and include actions taken.
[H3] 4.8 Record Maintenance and Security
[LI] All documentation is maintained securely within Passage Health
[LI] Access to records is restricted to authorized personnel only
[LI] All documentation must comply with HIPAA and confidentiality requirements
[H3] 4.9 Record Retention
[P] All service documentation will be retained for a minimum of 7 years, in accordance with Minnesota DHS requirements.
[H2] 5. Responsibility
[LI] Clinical Staff (L1, L2, L3 Providers): Responsible for accurate, timely, and complete documentation
[LI] Clinical Director (Kelly Ann Clark): Oversees documentation standards, audits, and compliance
[LI] Billing Manager (Mohammed Mohamed): Ensures documentation supports claims and billing accuracy
[H2] 6. Compliance and Enforcement
[P] Failure to comply with documentation requirements may result in:
[LI] Delayed or denied billing
[LI] Corrective action plans
[LI] Increased supervision
[LI] Disciplinary action, up to and including termination
[P] Serious violations may be reported to DHS or regulatory agencies.

[P] Service quality must be continuously monitored, evaluated, and documented using objective data, supervision records, and outcome-based measures. All services must demonstrate:
[LI] Ongoing medical necessity

[LI] Establish a structured system for documenting and evaluating service quality

[LI] Accuracy and completeness of documentation

[P] MALTREATMENT OF VULNERABLE ADULTS MANDATED REPORTING
[H1] 1. Purpose Statement
[P] The purpose of this policy is to establish standards and procedures for recognizing, reporting, documenting, and responding to suspected maltreatment of vulnerable adults receiving services through Bright Minds Therapy, L.L.C. DBA Noor Therapy Center (“Noor Therapy Center”).
[P] This policy exists to:
[LI] Protect vulnerable adults from abuse, neglect, financial exploitation, and harm
[LI] Ensure compliance with Minnesota mandated reporting laws and DHS requirements
[LI] Establish staff responsibilities for reporting suspected maltreatment
[LI] Promote immediate intervention and protective action
[LI] Maintain safe, ethical, and trauma-informed service environments
[LI] Ensure organizational accountability and compliance with state regulations
[H1] 2. Scope
[P] This policy applies to all Noor Therapy Center employees, contractors, interns, trainees, volunteers, and service providers who provide services to vulnerable adults, including but not limited to:
[LI] Behavioral Technicians (BTs)
[LI] Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3 providers
[LI] Qualified Supervising Professionals (QSPs)
[LI] Clinical Director
[LI] Administrative staff
[LI] Telehealth providers
[LI] Support staff with access to client information
[P] This policy applies in all service settings, including:
[LI] Client homes
[LI] Community-based services
[LI] Clinic-based services
[LI] Telehealth services
[LI] Transportation or transitional settings
[LI] Any environment where services are provided
[P] This policy applies whenever staff witness, suspect, receive disclosure of, or have reason to believe that a vulnerable adult has experienced maltreatment.
[H1] 3. Definitions
[P] Definitions referenced in this policy follow Minnesota Statutes sections 626.557 and 626.5572.
[H2] Vulnerable Adult
[P] A vulnerable adult is an individual age 18 or older who:
[LI] Receives services from a licensed facility or provider; or
[LI] Possesses physical, mental, emotional, or developmental impairments that limit the individual’s ability to protect themselves from maltreatment.
[H2] Maltreatment
[P] “Maltreatment” includes:
[LI] Abuse
[LI] Neglect
[LI] Financial exploitation
[H2] Abuse
[P] Any act that causes physical pain, injury, emotional distress, sexual abuse, unreasonable confinement, or deprivation of services necessary to maintain physical or mental health.
[H2] Neglect
[P] Failure or omission by a caregiver or service provider to provide care, supervision, food, clothing, shelter, health care, or services necessary to maintain the vulnerable adult’s health or safety.
[H2] Financial Exploitation
[P] The unauthorized or improper use of a vulnerable adult’s funds, property, assets, or resources.
[H2] Mandated Reporter
[P] A mandated reporter is an individual legally required to report suspected maltreatment of a vulnerable adult under Minnesota law.
[H1] 4. Policy Statement
[P] Noor Therapy Center maintains a zero-tolerance policy regarding maltreatment of vulnerable adults.
[P] All staff are mandated reporters and are legally required to immediately report known or suspected maltreatment of vulnerable adults in accordance with Minnesota law.
[P] Reports must be made immediately, and no later than 24 hours after the incident or suspicion arises.
[P] Staff are not responsible for investigating or proving maltreatment before making a report. Reasonable suspicion alone requires reporting.
[P] Noor Therapy Center prohibits retaliation against any individual who makes a good-faith report or participates in an investigation.
[P] Failure to comply with this policy may result in disciplinary action, termination, licensing consequences, and/or criminal penalties.
[H1] 5. Roles and Responsibilities
[H2] All Staff
[P] All employees and contractors are responsible for:
[LI] Recognizing signs of maltreatment
[LI] Immediately reporting suspected maltreatment
[LI] Maintaining confidentiality
[LI] Completing required training
[LI] Cooperating with investigations
[LI] Following documentation requirements
[H2] Direct Service Providers
[P] Direct care and service staff are responsible for:
[LI] Monitoring client safety during services
[LI] Reporting concerns observed during home, clinic, telehealth, or community sessions
[LI] Responding appropriately to immediate safety concerns
[LI] Completing factual documentation
[H2] Clinical Director/QSP
[P] The Clinical Director/QSP is responsible for:
[LI] Providing policy oversight
[LI] Supporting staff with reporting procedures
[LI] Monitoring staff compliance
[LI] Conducting or assigning internal reviews
[LI] Ensuring corrective actions are implemented
[LI] Maintaining policy accessibility
[H2] Administrative Leadership
[P] Administrative leadership is responsible for:
[LI] Maintaining training records
[LI] Supporting DHS compliance requirements
[LI] Monitoring policy implementation
[LI] Maintaining documentation standards
[H1] 6. Procedures and Guidelines
[H2] 6.1 Recognition of Maltreatment
[P] Staff must remain alert to indicators of abuse, neglect, or exploitation, including but not limited to:
[LI] Unexplained injuries
[LI] Sudden behavioral or emotional changes
[LI] Fearfulness around caregivers
[LI] Unsafe living conditions
[LI] Malnutrition or poor hygiene
[LI] Lack of necessary medical care
[LI] Unusual financial transactions or missing belongings
[LI] Isolation or unreasonable restraint
[LI] Signs of emotional abuse or intimidation
[H2] 6.2 Immediate Safety Response
[P] If a vulnerable adult is in immediate danger:
[LI] Staff must immediately call 911
[LI] Staff must prioritize client safety
[LI] Emergency procedures must be followed
[H2] 6.3 External Reporting Requirements
[P] If you are a mandated reporter and know or suspect maltreatment of a vulnerable adult, you must report it immediately and no later than 24 hours.
[H3] Minnesota Adult Abuse Reporting Center (MAARC)
[P] Phone: 844-880-1574
[P] Reports may also be made internally to:
[P] Kelly Ann Clark, Clinical Director/QSP
[P] If the person listed above is involved in the alleged or suspected maltreatment, reports must be made to:
[P] Agency Ownership or Designated Administrative Leadership
[H2] 6.4 Internal Reporting Process
[P] When an internal report is received, the Clinical Director/QSP is responsible for determining whether the report must be forwarded to the Minnesota Adult Abuse Reporting Center (MAARC).
[P] If the Clinical Director/QSP is involved in the alleged maltreatment, Agency Ownership or Designated Administrative Leadership will assume responsibility for determining whether the report must be forwarded.
[P] Any required external report must be forwarded within 24 hours.
[P] If a staff member reports internally, they will receive written notice within two working days indicating whether the report was forwarded to MAARC. The notice must protect the reporter’s identity and inform the reporter that they may independently contact MAARC if dissatisfied with the internal determination.
[H2] 6.5 Documentation Requirements
[P] Staff must document:
[LI] Objective observations
[LI] Date and time of observations
[LI] Date and time the report was made
[LI] Agency contacted
[LI] Name of intake worker, if available
[LI] Report or reference number, if available
[LI] Protective actions taken
[P] Documentation must remain factual and free from assumptions or personal opinions.
[H2] 6.6 Confidentiality
[P] All reports and related documentation are confidential.
[P] Information may only be shared with:
[LI] Investigative agencies
[LI] DHS licensing representatives
[LI] Authorized Noor Therapy Center leadership
[LI] Individuals directly involved in the investigation process
[P] Unauthorized disclosure of information is prohibited.
[H2] 6.7 Internal Review
[P] When Noor Therapy Center has reason to know that an internal or external report of alleged or suspected maltreatment has been made, the organization will complete an internal review within 30 calendar days.
[P] The review will evaluate whether:
[LI] Related policies and procedures were followed
[LI] Policies and procedures were adequate
[LI] Additional staff training is needed
[LI] The event is similar to past incidents
[LI] Corrective action is necessary to protect vulnerable adults
[H2] 6.8 Primary and Secondary Persons Responsible for Internal Review
[P] The internal review will be completed by:
[P] Kelly Ann Clark, Clinical Director/QSP
[P] If this individual is involved in the alleged or suspected maltreatment, the review will be completed by:
[P] Agency Ownership or Designated Administrative Leadership
[H2] 6.9 Documentation of Internal Review
[P] Noor Therapy Center will document completion of all internal reviews and make documentation immediately accessible to the Commissioner upon request.
[H2] 6.10 Corrective Action Plan
[P] Based on the results of the internal review, Noor Therapy Center will develop, document, and implement corrective action plans designed to:
[LI] Correct current lapses
[LI] Improve safety practices
[LI] Prevent future incidents
[LI] Improve staff compliance and performance
